## What LiteServer Actually Is

LiteServer is a Dutch hosting provider offering VPS, managed hosting, and shared web hosting. Their infrastructure is located outside Amsterdam — which is one of the best-connected spots in Europe for network performance. They run on AMD and Intel hardware with SuperMicro server chassis, and use Juniper networking gear.

Everything runs on KVM. No OpenVZ, no container tricks. You get a real virtual machine, with root access, your choice of OS (Debian, Ubuntu, AlmaLinux, FreeBSD, CloudLinux, and more), and a 1 Gbps network connection.

The thing that sets them apart from a lot of budget hosts? No overselling. They actually say this on their website — and based on years of community benchmarks on LowEndTalk, it holds up. Users have reported server uptimes measured in *years*, not days.

## What Happens at Bandwidth Limit?

If you hit your monthly traffic cap, your VPS stays online but gets throttled to 10 Mbps for the rest of the month. You can buy additional bandwidth blocks (€2.50 per 5 TB, one-time or monthly) to restore full 1 Gbps speed. Counters reset on the first of each month.
